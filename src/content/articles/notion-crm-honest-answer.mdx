Yes, Notion can do CRM. The real question is whether it's the right fit for your business.

After using Notion as a CRM for over a year — tracking clients, deals, and follow-ups — here's what I've found.

## What Notion handles well

**Contact and company management**
Notion databases are excellent for storing and organising contact information. You can build exactly the fields you need, create filtered views, and link contacts to companies, deals, and notes.

**Pipeline visualisation**
The Kanban board view transforms any database into a visual pipeline. Drag cards between stages. Filter by owner, value, or close date.

**Notes and meeting history**
Notion's rich text support means you can store detailed notes, meeting summaries, and action items directly on a contact or deal record.

**Flexible reporting**
Filter and sort your databases to answer business questions. What's the total value of proposals sent this month? Which clients haven't been contacted in 30 days? Notion can surface this.

**Document storage**
Proposals, contracts, and presentations can live in Notion pages linked directly to client records. No separate file management system needed.

## What Notion doesn't do

**Email tracking**
Notion won't log your emails automatically. You have to manually record email interactions. This is the biggest gap versus dedicated CRM software.

**Built-in automation**
Notion has some basic automation features, but you'll need n8n or Zapier for meaningful workflow automation — like sending follow-up reminders or logging new leads automatically.

**Phone and call logging**
No native call logging. Some people integrate with tools like Aircall or simply log calls manually.

**Reporting dashboards**
Notion's native reporting is limited. You can filter and sort, but you can't build the kind of charts and dashboards that dedicated CRMs offer.

## The setup that works

After testing different approaches, this structure works best for most small businesses:

**Contacts database** — All people you do business with
**Companies database** — Organisations, linked to contacts
**Deals database** — Opportunities, linked to contacts and companies
**Interactions log** — Every call, email, and meeting (optional but valuable)

Link them with Relations and Rollups so everything is connected.

Add n8n automation to:
- Auto-create contact records from web forms
- Send daily follow-up reminders to WhatsApp
- Log completed deals to a Notion reporting page

## The honest verdict

Notion is a legitimate CRM for businesses with straightforward sales processes and budgets that don't stretch to HubSpot or Salesforce.

It won't replace enterprise CRM software. It will replace the spreadsheet you're currently using as a CRM — and do it better.

The time investment to set it up is 2–3 hours. The ongoing cost is zero.

If you're already using Notion for other work, building your CRM there is a no-brainer. Everything lives in one workspace, and the context switching disappears.

Start simple. You can always add more structure as your needs become clearer.
